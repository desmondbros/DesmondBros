We Made a Commercial At Home During Quarantine - Lockdown Creativity at Home

08 May 2020

Estimated read time: 4 - 5 mins.

How we kept creative at home during COVID-19 Lockdown

Lockdown means no shoots. Our trigger fingers started getting itchy for a record button to press. We decided to keep the creative muscles exercised by making a commercial from scratch at home, in one day.

 

The Challenge

 

Seeing as we were stuck in the house, we couldn’t rent any extra equipment that we might use on normal productions. We decided to limit ourselves to using just our camera, two lights and our sound gear.

 

But what to make a video about? We needed to find a pretend client. Behance is full of amazing projects designed by creators from all over the world. We found a cool app concept for finding a great restaurant. Maybe we were just hungry, and wishfully thinking of life after quarantine, but it seemed like the perfect choice.

 

Then - we just followed the same steps we always take for video production!

 

1. Plan

 

We drew up a treatment of what we imagined the video to be. A plan or a script is always essential for producing a great video. It’s important to show the client exactly what you’re thinking (even our imaginary client for this project!)

 

2. Production Design

 

No matter how fancy the camera, it’s what is in front of the lens that makes or breaks an image. That’s where production design comes in (basically the way props, set and costumes come together to create a feel for a film).

 

We were limited to our house, so we decided to reinvent Cian’s bedroom to look great on screen. We worked with a lot of primary colours. Sky blue walls, red locker, yellow props. Any item that was going to be on screen, we arranged it to look neat and in place.

3. Shoot

 

The exciting part of a video production - getting this rocket off the ground.

 

Normally, we wouldn’t be in front of camera, but… Lockdown Filming! Cian took the bullet this time, while Jack set up the lighting. He used two LED lights, as well as taking advantage of the sun. He placed diffusion gel on the windows to control the brightness of the sunlight.

4. Voice over

 

We improvised a recording studio by throwing a blanket behind Cian’s head. Not just for snoozing!

 

5. Editing

 

Down to the nitty-gritty. Cian took the footage into the home video editing suite, and got to work. He used a lot of motion graphics, such as the text bubbles. He did some hard work in After Effects to create the graphics we wanted.

 

Sound design, final pass at editing, colour grading in DaVinci Resolve - and it was finished. Here’s the final product!

It was a fun project to do to keep busy and challenge ourselves, while keeping the creative juices flowing. This is also the workflow we use to do all of our video shoots!
